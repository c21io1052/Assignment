Palace Skateboards raided Elton John's wardrobe for its spring collection

Elton John is as known for his eclectic style as he is for his music. Since rising to fame in 1969 following the success of his breakthrough album Empty Sky, John (AKA Reginald Kenneth Dwight) has rocked more iconic lewks than you could shake a tambourine at. The “Emperor's New Clothes” superstar has impressed in everything from bedazzled, sequinned and flared jumpsuits to high shine Versace satin suits. Then, of course, there's the iconic Bob Mackie-designed, crystal-embellished baseball costume he wore on stage at the Dodgers Stadium in 1976, which Harry Styles replicated for Halloween in 2018.

How the Bearbrick became streetwear’s most enduring icon

Every few weeks in the mid-aughts, a “crazy, crazy” car—a 2006 Bentley Flying Spur, to be precise—would pull up to Mobius, Wonhee Lee’s half-gallery half-streetwear store in Los Angeles. Lee knew the driver well, not just because he was the famed celebrity jeweler Ben Yang, better known as Ben Baller, but because the two had developed a predictable routine. A half-dozen roughly two-foot-high boxes would come in the mail. Lee would give his loyal customer a call, and Yang would speed over in his Bentley. In the boxes? Bearbricks (formally Be@rbrick), the massive Japanese toy company Medicom signature item, and one that’s had its paw fully in the honeypots of streetwear, art, and design over the past 18 years.

Is this Supreme's most gentlemanly season yet?

If you've ever walked through London's Soho towards Carnaby Street, you'll have most likely clocked the queues of highly branded young guys regularly waiting to get inside the sleek, minimal Supreme store on Peter Street. And while the brand has become near-legendary with skatewear fans, this season Supreme seems to be taking a more gentlemanly turn. For proof, take a look at the Autumn/Winter 2017 lookbook below - including a couple of exclusive extras right at the end for GQ superfans. Sure those bold, brash trademark logos are still there (as well as the brand's new collab with Nineties sportwear legend Champion), but there's also plenty for the man who's not heading for a halfpipe: a cool shearling collared leather Harrington, knitted polos, a checked wool topcoat. Is Supreme is growing up? Sure looks that way. The entire line hits the web on Thursday 25 August, but you scope out all the individual items on supremenewyork.com right now.
